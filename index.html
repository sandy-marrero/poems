Poemario de Sandy Marrero

A collection of my emotions

Autobiography

I am Sandy Marrero, a Software Engineering student. Art has been a part of my life for as long as I can remember. I wrote these poems 4 years ago, at the start of the COVID pandemic. This poems are an encapsulation of all that I felt, saw and thought. Hope you enjoy, feel and relate to them as much as I do.

The 3AM Duet

A gloom follows me around, taking turns with storms to make my thoughts fly by. All they do is faking I’m miserable. Just fine I’m getting by. They follow as a couple, destined to wreck havoc. I take out my bag and bring out my greatest weapon, my phone. As if allergic they vanish. Silently the night arrives and so they do. It’s 3AM, they crawl into my ears, “you are horrible,” “you deserve no one,” “you are a terrible person”. The quotation ticks don’t leave my side, slowly growing as insecurities exude out of my pores. I’m changing, my confident self is being taken hostage and what’s left is a child afraid of living. A child infested with insecurities, incapable of handling the world.

The New Life

Sometimes life gets too monotonous, as I wish I could turn the tide around. Too deep into it I notice, seems like my life sails itself into oblivion. A shipwreck is what I need, to take the mantle of captain of my ship. However a new sail I got to make Made out of strong will, capable of sailing through the hardest of life.

Empty

A reflection of what’s behind is what I see in my chest, to the left. A void into sadness, which doesn’t discriminate, taking all of you, it is a theft. You can’t help but hide it but it digs deeper into what’s left. No choice to keep going, he is one day leaving, either with or without me. He is me.

Letter to my future self

Don’t be so hard on yourself, You are being your best self. Life is not easy, but you have What it takes to keep thriving. Hopefully you will achieve your dreams, That professional you always wanted to be. Giving back to your family for their sacrifices, Giving back to the world for pushing you harder. Your past self looks up to you, It’s up to you to make him proud. You are more capable than you think, Your experiences made you stronger. Cheers to the future, Cheers to my future self, Sandy

First Thoughts

I opened my eyes to a new world, Right in the middle of the living room. My life appeared to had started as I Could not recognize anything around me. A nice lady arrives and finds me, I could not help but wonder who she was. My mother is what she said, seems like alone I am not. Maybe I started living, Or I forgot everything. A dream could it be? I will never know, but these are my first memories.
